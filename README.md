# 📊 China Deflation Analysis

An analytical study of China’s post-pandemic deflation, highlighting the paradox of rapid money-supply growth amid stagnant or falling prices.  
The project integrates quantitative analysis in Python (via Jupyter Notebook) with visual and narrative interpretation presented in the accompanying report and slides.

## 🧠 Key Insights

- Despite M2 growth exceeding **50% since 2020**, both CPI and PPI show persistent weakness or decline.  
- Rising **precautionary savings** and declining **housing confidence** have increased real money demand.  
- **Youth unemployment** and low consumer confidence indicate a structural erosion of growth expectations.  
- The deflationary trend reflects behavioral and institutional dynamics — not monetary tightening alone.
